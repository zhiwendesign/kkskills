# KKSticker No-Line Design Rules

## Core Mood

The visual language should feel:

- playful
- airy
- sticky
- soft but structured
- readable

It should not feel:

- corporate
- heavy outlined comic
- warm scrapbook handmade
- retro game HUD

## Palette

Use this order of importance:

1. `#2f78ff`, `#3f86ff`, `#eaf4ff` as the blue grid environment
2. `#fffdf7` / `#ffffff` as the mascot board surface
3. `#d9ebff`, `#f7efba`, `#edf8db`, `#ffffff` as soft card colors
4. `#314253` / `rgba(49,66,83,0.5)` as softened text and edge color
5. `#ff8d4c` only as a small shadow or warm accent

## Layout Bias

The default `3:4` no-line kksticker poster should feel like a softened mascot whiteboard:

- one rounded white board sitting on a visible blue grid background
- one mascot-like top silhouette or ear shape
- one tag row near the upper-left
- one date sticker near the upper-right
- one highlight side card
- one full-width summary strip
- two main content panels with stacked rounded cards
- one bottom badge rail
- one lifted footer note area

## Signature Behavior

- let the board silhouette feel friendly and memorable
- keep the grid background visible around the board edges
- make tags, date chips, side cards, and footer badges feel like stickers
- use soft borders and shadows instead of thick black outlines
- keep the title heavy and large even when the surrounding edges are gentler

## Date Sticker

- keep it near the upper-right
- use `YYYY年MM月DD日`
- let it feel like a pinned label, not a plain chip

## Contact Cluster

- use `assets/contact-code.png` and `assets/kk-character.jpeg`
- keep both cards the same size
- place the QR to the left of the IP
- keep `合作交流` and `是卡卡呀` as compact captions below

## Final Check

- the mascot kksticker feeling is obvious
- the title and side card establish hierarchy quickly
- the blue grid background remains visible
- the date sticker is visible and correctly formatted
- the QR and Kaka IP are both present
- the two contact cards are the same size
- text stays readable despite the softer boundaries
