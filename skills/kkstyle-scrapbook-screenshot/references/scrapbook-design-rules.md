# Scrapbook Design Rules

## Core Mood

The visual language should feel:

- warm
- handmade
- editorial but friendly
- layered
- easy to scan

It should not feel:

- cold corporate
- glassy tech UI
- futuristic neon
- luxury minimal

## Palette

Use this order of importance:

1. `#efe2cc` / `#f5ead7` as the paper-toned background
2. `#fff8ef` / `#fff3e0` as main paper surfaces
3. `#51463b` for softened outlines and text
4. `#b77a4a`, `#8e9b63`, `#d5845a` as accent colors
5. small amounts of red pencil, olive, or tape yellow when needed

## Paper Language

The poster should look like a layered paper board:

- one main paper sheet
- one or two rotated support sheets behind it
- taped note labels or clipped summary strips
- a highlighted paper card near the upper-right

Use shadows and borders to suggest physical layers.
Do not overuse decoration until the content becomes secondary.

## Layout Bias

The default `3:4` poster should feel full, not empty:

- one main title note
- one highlighted number or takeaway card
- one summary strip
- two main information sheets
- one lifted footer note area

The title should not feel jammed:

- give it breathing room below the top labels
- allow slightly looser line-height on two-line titles
- keep the footer notes visibly lifted

## Date Label

Every poster should include a date label:

- place it near the upper-right
- let it sit on or near the paper edge
- use `YYYY年MM月DD日`
- keep it cute and readable, not louder than the highlight card

## Contact Cluster

Every poster should include the bundled contact cluster:

- use `assets/contact-code.png` as the QR card
- use `assets/kk-character.jpeg` as the IP card
- place the QR to the left of the IP
- keep both cards the same size
- keep `合作交流` and `是卡卡呀` as restrained captions below
- keep the whole cluster above nearby paper edges

## Typography

- Use a warmer serif or editorial heading feel when useful.
- Body text should remain clean and readable.
- Labels should feel like notes, tabs, or paper strips.
- Titles should be short and dense.

## Content Rules

- One title note should dominate the page.
- Support points should be compact and grouped.
- Long source material must be summarized before layout.
- If the page feels empty, add another paper strip or note block.
- If the page feels too busy, reduce decoration before reducing content hierarchy.

## Final Check

Before returning HTML, verify:

- the scrapbook collage feeling is obvious
- the date label is visible and correctly formatted
- the QR and Kaka IP are both present
- the two contact cards are the same size
- the footer notes feel lifted, not sunk to the bottom edge
- text remains the main payload
- the page still works as a screenshot, not as a scrolling web page
